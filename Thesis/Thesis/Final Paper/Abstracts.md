# V1
This paper addresses the problem of 3D object modeling using a single monocular camera by proposing a Next Best View (NBV) approach for data acquisition in photogrammetry. Unlike existing NBV methods that rely on volumetric representations, our framework leverages depth maps derived from deep learning–based depth estimation. We argue that volumetric approaches can be computationally expensive and may overlook fine geometric details of complex objects, limiting reconstruction accuracy. In contrast, our method instantiates candidate viewpoints by analyzing depth-derived geometrical features, such as edges or regions of high depth variation. We validate our approach within a realistic virtual environment built using Unity HDRP, where virtual 3D models serve as ground truth. To assess the quality of the reconstructions, we employ both the Hausdorff distance on selected object sections and Euclidean distance over the entire reconstruction. Our experimental results show that leveraging pixel-wise depth information yields more accurate and computationally efficient reconstructions than volumetric methods, highlighting the effectiveness of our depth-based NBV strategy for single-camera photogrammetry.

# V2
This paper addresses the challenge of 3D object modeling with a single monocular camera by proposing a Next Best View (NBV) approach for data acquisition in photogrammetry. Although many NBV methods rely on volumetric representations, such approaches can be computationally intensive and may struggle to capture the fine geometric details of complex objects. Instead, our framework leverages depth maps derived from a deep learning–based depth estimator to guide the selection of new viewpoints. Specifically, we identify geometric features (e.g., edges or regions of high depth variation) from the depth map and generate candidate views accordingly. We validate our method within a realistic virtual environment built using Unity HDRP, using virtual 3D models as ground truth. To assess the quality of the reconstructions, we employ both Hausdorff distance for selected object sections and Euclidean distance over the entire reconstruction. Our experimental results show that utilizing pixel-wise depth information in a depth-based NBV strategy can offer accurate and computationally efficient reconstructions, providing a promising alternative to more resource-intensive approaches for single-camera photogrammetry.
