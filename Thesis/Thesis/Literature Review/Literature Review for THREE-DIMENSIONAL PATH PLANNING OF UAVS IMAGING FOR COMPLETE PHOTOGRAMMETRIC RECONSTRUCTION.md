This method focuses on capturing images that enable complete and precise photogrammetric 3D reconstructions. It takes into account a coarse model from an initial flight as prior knowledge to estimate its completeness and precision​​.
2. **Integration of Photogrammetric Constraints in View Planning**: The study incorporates photogrammetric constraints within the framework of model-based view planning. This involves analyzing the completeness and precision of the coarse input model and planning additional viewpoints to enhance the quality of the 3D model. <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">_Considerations include camera geometry constraints like baseline and observing angle, as well as the error ellipsoid of each observed point, to guide the view planning_</mark>. This approach allows for optimization of each viewpoint separately, aiming to achieve better completeness and precision​​.
3. **Improved Results in Real-World Experiment**: The paper reports on a real-world experiment conducted on a ship lock, demonstrating that the proposed method can achieve a more complete result with similar precision compared to an existing 3D planning method. #### Checking for Holes
Holes are checked by comparing the normals from the Point Cloud and its corresponding triangulated Mesh. For a consistent point cloud it is expected that the angle between it's normal and that of a corresponding point in the mesh to be low. This can be checked throught the dot product property of Vectors.
