Photogrammetric constraints are guidelines or rules that ensure the accuracy and reliability of photogrammetric processes.
1. **Camera Geometry Constraints**: These involve the spatial relationship between the camera and the object being photographed, including factors like the baseline (distance between camera positions), observing angle (angle at which the camera views the object), and coverage area. These factors are critical for ensuring that the images captured contain sufficient overlap and perspectives to accurately reconstruct the 3D model.
2. **Image Quality Constraints**: These refer to the requirements for image resolution, focus, and clarity. High-quality images are essential for precise measurements and detailed reconstructions.
3. **Overlap and Redundancy**: Adequate overlap between images is necessary for stereo matching and 3D reconstruction. This often requires a certain percentage of overlap between consecutive images.
4. **Lighting and Environmental Conditions**: Proper lighting is crucial for capturing detailed images. Environmental factors like weather and time of day can also affect image quality and are considered in outdoor photogrammetric surveys.
