Think of an objective function like the score in a game. Your goal in a game is to either maximize your score (like in basketball where more points are better) or minimize it (like in golf where fewer strokes are better). In both cases, your actions in the game (shooting hoops or swinging the club) are aimed at achieving this goal.

In mathematical problems and computer science, an objective function works similarly. It's like a formula or rule that tells you how good your "game" is based on your decisions or actions. You try to make choices that either make this score as high as possible (maximizing) or as low as possible (minimizing), depending on what the "game" (or your problem) requires.
