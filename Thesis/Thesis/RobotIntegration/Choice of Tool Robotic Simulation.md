The tools at my  disposal are Coppeliasim and Webots.
One paper seems to suggest that Coppeliasim has better  performance(accuracy) than Webots.
Another paper says Webots produces more reproducable results, Coppeliasim is lighter.
Webots seems to be more feature packed than Copelliasim.
The categorization in Copelliasim is better with a category of mobile and non-mobile robots.
Coepelliasim has more Robot arm options. However some of them are Parallel robots.

There are ROS packages that can be used out of the box for various controls as seen from [[audonnetSystematicComparisonSimulation2022 ]] [[farleyHowPickMobile2022]] where in the first, movements for moving the Robot arm are proposesd, while the second talks of packages for moving the Mobile Robot.
Both Copelliasim and Webots have plugins for interfacing with ROS.
Copelliasim is generally more accurate than Webots
